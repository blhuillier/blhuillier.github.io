Benjamin L'Huillier | Cosmologist, Assistant Professor

French cosmologist in South Korea

About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Sejong University in Seoul, South Korea. I use advanced statistical methods to test the concordance model of cosmology and its underlying hypotheses using state-of-the-art data and simulations.

My research is supported by the National Research Foundation of Korea. I explore the limits of the concordance ΛCDM model using N-body simulations—both within and beyond the standard model—and apply advanced statistical methods to test its assumptions. I was also a member of the DESI collaboration, contributing to the time domain and the cosmological simulations working groups.

Links: Google Scholar · GitHub · Twitter · LinkedIn

Science Journey

I completed my PhD at LUX, Paris Observatory and Paris Sorbonne University, working in the Galaxies and Cosmology group with Françoise Combes and Benoit Semelin, as part of the Momentum project. I also taught physics and data analysis as a teaching assistant at Université Paris Cité.

Since 2012, I have been based in South Korea. I was a Research Fellow at the Korea Institute for Advanced Study (KIAS), working with Profs. Changbom Park and Juhan Kim on cosmological structure formation using simulations such as Horizon Run 4. I then joined the CosKASI group at the Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute working with Arman Shafieloo, before becoming Research Professor and W. C. Rufus Fellow in the Yonsei Observable UNiverse Group (YOUNG).
