Research

From forces that shape cosmic expansion to the birth of galaxies

Overview

I study structure formation, from galaxies to the large-scale structures of the Universe. I use cosmological simulations and advanced statistical tools to answer questions such as:

  • How do galaxies form and evolve?
  • What drives the accelerated expansion of the Universe?
  • Is Einstein's General Relativity the correct description of gravity?
  • What are the initial conditions of the Universe?

My complete list of publications: NASA/ADS · InSPIRE/HEP · Google Scholar · ORCID: 0000-0003-2934-6243

Testing the Concordance Model of Cosmology

Is the Universe isotropic and homogeneous? Is the metric FLRW? What is the nature of Dark Energy — is it a cosmological constant? What is the curvature of the Universe?

I apply advanced statistical methods to current and upcoming survey data to test the assumptions of the standard ΛCDM model.

Cosmology Beyond the Concordance Model

What is dark energy? Is gravity correctly described by Einstein's General Theory of Relativity? Is the primordial power spectrum a power law? I explore modifications to the standard model through both simulations and observational tests.

Cosmological Simulations

I use cosmological simulations to study the evolution of galaxies and dark matter halos within the Universe's large-scale structure. I contributed to the design and analysis of Horizon Run 4.

Galaxy Formation & Evolution

How do galaxies assemble their mass? How do galaxies evolve within the large-scale structure? I study these questions using both N-body simulations and statistical comparisons with observational data.
